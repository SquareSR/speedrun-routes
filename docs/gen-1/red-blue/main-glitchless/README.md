## What route do I choose?
- Different routes exist to optimize how much time it takes to get a specific goal time. The way you play to get a sub 1:55 time in glitchless efficiently (sub 2:05 in classic) is much different than the way you play for a sub 1:47 in glitchless (sub 1:57 in classic). 
- Beginner routes are recommended if you are first starting out. They are focused towards allowing as many runs as possible to be completed, which is great for learning the game.
  - Note that you can also continue to use beginner routes even as you improve. Times as low as 1:47 in glitchless and 1:59 in classic have been obtained using versions of beginner routes.
- Intermediate routes are focused toward goal times where dying would make PBs unlikely/impossible, so they cut out revives. These routes still plays safer than the advanced/wr routes, namely skipping highly risky strategies such as Silph Red Bar. The intermediate routes were written with goal times around 1:47 or slower for glitchless (1:59 or slower for classic) in mind. 
- The advanced routes are mainly focused toward goal times of 1:45/1:46 or better in glitchless (1:55-1:57 or better in classic), where you are likely to have to consistently go for more risky red bar strategies.
- Turning solely to glitchless - the WR Route is focused toward goal times of 1:44/1:43, where you must go for risky red bar strategies and have a riskier Agatha and Champ fight. Note that runners recently have favored the Advanced Flex Route over the WR route when performing 1:44-or-better attempts.
- We reccomend that new players start off with whichever category they prefer; however, it is worth noting that the classic beginner route does contain more embedded maps to assist runners who have not played red in a long time. The glitchless route does assume that you already know how to beat the game normally and that you understand [how RNG manips work in gen 1](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QirPrbub21g).
